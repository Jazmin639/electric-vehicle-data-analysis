# Electric Vehicle Population Data Analysis

This project presents a comprehensive, data-driven visual analysis of the evolution, geographical distribution, and market composition of the Electric Vehicle (EV) sector. Utilizing modern Business Intelligence frameworks, the core objective is to identify technology adoption patterns and manufacturer dominance within the green energy and sustainable mobility landscape.

## 🔑 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The analytical panel centrally monitors the current state of the evaluated fleet:
*   **Total Vehicles:** A massive global fleet of **127,590** registered units.
*   **Average Electric Range:** An overall mean of **74.46 Miles**, a critical metric for evaluating historical battery capacity and efficiency changes.
*   **Fleet Segmentation:**
    *   **BEV (Battery Electric Vehicles):** 104,912 units (**82.2%** market share), demonstrating a commanding preference for 100% pure electric technology.
    *   **PHEV (Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles):** 22,678 units (**17.8%** market share), operating as a transitional plug-in hybrid option.

---

## 📈 Key Insights Extracted

1.  **Exponential Temporal Growth:** The area chart displays an extraordinary acceleration in EV adoption starting in 2021, reaching its historical record in **2023 with over 28.5K new vehicles**, followed by a stabilization phase in the current transition cycle.
2.  **Absolute Market Dominance:** **TESLA** heavily spearheads the sector with **68,983 vehicles**, commanding **54.07%** of the entire market analyzed. It is followed at a substantial distance by Nissan (10.58%) and Chevrolet (5.97%).
3.  **Incentive Eligibility (CAFV):** The donut chart reveals that a prominent volume falls under *CAFV Unknown* (60.6K) versus *CAFV Eligible* (55.9K), indicating a significant opportunity for further policy and clean energy subsidy optimization analysis.
